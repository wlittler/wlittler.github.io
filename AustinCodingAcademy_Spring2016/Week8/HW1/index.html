Tesla Motors offers the Model S, Model X, and Model 3 in a variety of configurations and price points. Easily the most attractive (and expensive) electric vehicles available.
Nissan offers the Leaf EV, one of the lowest-cost fully electric vehicles available in the US. With incentives, it can cost under $20,000.
Chevrolet offers the Volt, a hybrid that can operate in full-electric mode, for those that don't want to fully commit to electric.

Three main factors that affect which EV fits your lifestyle: charging speed, range, and cost.

Charging (from 0-100%)

  • Tesla - 45 minutes to ~20 hours*
  • Nissan Leaf - 30 minutes to 26 hours*
  • Chevy Volt - 4.5 hours to 13 hours**
*Dependent on charge source
**Chevy Volt can also charge via it's on-board gasoline generator

Range**

  • Tesla - 310 to 240 miles*
  • Nissan Leaf - 87 to 104 miles
  • Chevy Volt - 53 (battery only) to 420 (gas-assisted)
*Dependent on battery configuration
**Rated, actual depends on driving habirs

Cost

  • Tesla - $35,000+*
  • Nissan Leaf - $29,010+
  • Chevy Volt - $33,200+
*Model 3 starts @ $35,000, Model S/X @ $70,000
