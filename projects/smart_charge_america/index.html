Level 2 Chargers


Level 2 charging stations are equipped with a 240V, AC plug which requires professional installation. Level 2 charging stations can operate at up to 100A and 20kW however, most home Level 2 stations will operate at lower frequencies (30A transmitting 7.2kW). Level 2 comes equipped with the standard J1772 connector and based on the battery type and circuit capacity, can add up to 10 to 20 miles of range to a PEV per hour of charging time. Thus, a standard 40 mile range PHEV can take from 4 to 2 hrs of charge time to reach a full charge. Level 2 charging stations can be purchased between $500.00 to $7,000.00 (installed) before any federal and/or state tax credit incentives are received. The more basic Level 2 charging stations are equipped with your more standard safety features and status lights and the more advanced “SMART” level 2 products have features such as charging timers, enhanced displays, communication capabilities and keypads. Most drivers will prefer Level 2 charging however, older homes may have insufficient electrical capacity which will require installation of new electric circuits to accommodate for its needed current.

FAQs


Does it matter when PEV’s charge?

Yes! Most of the electricity that you use come directly from the electrical provider’s electrical plants. Unlike deregulated electric service areas, regulated service providers, when demand is high, have to purchase extra electricity from the wholesale market. In doing so, the regulated electric provider pays more for the electricity when is then pass down to the end user. The demand for energy changes throughout the day. Due to lighter demands and usage during night or off-peak hours electricity providers will lower the price per kWhr which makes it even cheaper to charge your vehicle at night. Imagine if gas stations charged lower rates at night then during the day.

Are EVSE a concern for safety hazards?

Operational safety and product design are top priority amongst EVSE manufacturers. Your EVSE should come equipped with de-energizer triggers and an inter-locking cable connector feature, which allows electric current to distribute once everything is safely locked into place. If at anytime during charging an unpredictable outside source should cause a cable rupture or the disconnection of the connectors (J1772) the equipment is designed to shut off instantly.

I have an outlet in my garage and my PEV includes its own EVSE. Why do I need to purchase special charging equipment?

Most PEV’s/PHEV’s come with charging equipment for a standard 120-volt outlet. This type of equipment is commonly referred to as a Level 1 charging device. The equipment tab explains more in depth the difference in charging level characteristics, e.g. Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3 (DC).
